# Basic Math Concepts

Mathematics is the study of numbers, shapes, and patterns. In this document, we'll cover some fundamental concepts in math that are essential for understanding more advanced topics.

## 1. Numbers

### Types of Numbers
- **Natural Numbers**: These are the numbers used for counting. Example: `1, 2, 3, 4, ...`
- **Whole Numbers**: These include natural numbers plus zero. Example: `0, 1, 2, 3, ...`
- **Integers**: These include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero. Example: `-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...`
- **Rational Numbers**: Numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers. Example: `1/2, 3/4, -5/6`
- **Irrational Numbers**: Numbers that cannot be expressed as fractions. Example: `π (pi), √2`
- **Real Numbers**: All rational and irrational numbers combined.
- **Complex Numbers**: Numbers that include a real part and an imaginary part. Example: `3 + 4i`

## 2. Basic Arithmetic Operations

### Addition (+)
Addition is the process of finding the total or sum by combining two or more numbers.
- Example:  
  `5 + 3 = 8`

### Subtraction (−)
Subtraction is the process of taking one number away from another.
- Example:  
  `9 − 4 = 5`

### Multiplication (×)
Multiplication is the process of repeated addition.
- Example:  
  `4 × 3 = 12`

### Division (÷)
Division is the process of splitting a number into equal parts.
- Example:  
  `12 ÷ 4 = 3`

### Order of Operations (PEMDAS)
When solving math problems with multiple operations, follow the order of operations to ensure the correct result:
- **P**arentheses
- **E**xponents (Powers and Square Roots)
- **M**ultiplication and **D**ivision (from left to right)
- **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (from left to right)

Example:  
`2 + 3 × (4 + 5)`  
First, solve inside the parentheses: `4 + 5 = 9`  
Then, multiply: `3 × 9 = 27`  
Finally, add: `2 + 27 = 29`

## 3. Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers: the numerator (top number) and the denominator (bottom number).

- **Simplifying Fractions**: Dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD).
  - Example:  
    Simplify `6/8`: Divide both by 2 → `3/4`

- **Adding/Subtracting Fractions**: The fractions must have the same denominator.
  - Example:  
    `1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4`

- **Multiplying Fractions**: Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
  - Example:  
    `2/3 × 4/5 = 8/15`

- **Dividing Fractions**: Invert the second fraction and multiply.
  - Example:  
    `2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6`

## 4. Decimals

Decimals are another way to represent parts of a whole, using a decimal point. 

- Example:  
  `0.5 = 1/2`,  
  `0.75 = 3/4`

### Operations with Decimals
- **Addition/Subtraction**: Align the decimal points before performing the operation.
- **Multiplication**: Multiply as whole numbers, then place the decimal in the result.
- **Division**: Move the decimal point of the divisor to make it a whole number, then divide.

Example of adding decimals:  
`2.5 + 3.75 = 6.25`

## 5. Percentages

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It is often used to represent how much one number is of another number.

- **Converting a Decimal to a Percentage**: Multiply the decimal by 100.
  - Example:  
    `0.85 × 100 = 85%`
  
- **Converting a Percentage to a Decimal**: Divide the percentage by 100.
  - Example:  
    `25% = 25 ÷ 100 = 0.25`

- **Finding Percent of a Number**: Multiply the number by the percentage (in decimal form).
  - Example:  
    `50% of 200 = 0.5 × 200 = 100`

## 6. Algebra Basics

### Variables
In algebra, variables (usually represented by letters such as `x` or `y`) stand for unknown numbers.

### Solving Equations
An equation is a mathematical sentence that shows the equality between two expressions. To solve an equation, find the value of the variable that makes the equation true.

Example:  
Solve for `x`:  
`2x + 5 = 11`  
First, subtract 5 from both sides:  
`2x = 6`  
Then, divide both sides by 2:  
`x = 3`

## 7. Geometry Basics

### Points, Lines, and Angles
- **Point**: A location with no size or dimension.
- **Line**: A one-dimensional object extending infinitely in both directions.
- **Angle**: Formed by two rays with a common endpoint (vertex).

### Shapes
- **Circle**: A round shape with all points equidistant from the center.
- **Triangle**: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
- **Rectangle**: A four-sided shape with opposite sides equal and all angles 90°.
- **Square**: A rectangle with all sides equal in length.

## Conclusion

This document provides an overview of some fundamental math concepts. Mastering these basic topics is essential for understanding more advanced areas of mathematics like calculus, statistics, and number theory. Keep practicing, and you'll develop a stronger math foundation!
